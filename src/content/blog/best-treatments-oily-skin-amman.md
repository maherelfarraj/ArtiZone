# Best Treatments for Oily Skin in Amman: What Actually Controls Shine and Breakouts

Oily skin is one of the most common concerns we treat at ArtiZone — and one of the most mismanaged. The instinct is to strip the skin dry, use harsh cleansers, and apply mattifying products around the clock. The result is almost always the opposite of what you want: the skin produces even more oil to compensate, pores enlarge, and breakouts become more frequent.

Effective oily skin management is about regulation, not elimination. The goal is to bring sebum production to a balanced level — not to dry the skin out. This guide covers the professional treatments and home care strategies that actually achieve that in Amman's climate.

---

## Why Amman's Climate Makes Oily Skin Worse

Oily skin is driven by overactive sebaceous (oil) glands — but the degree of oiliness is significantly influenced by environment. Amman's climate creates a specific challenge:

- **Summer heat (June–September):** High temperatures increase sebum production. The skin produces more oil as a cooling mechanism and as a response to heat stress.
- **Dry winters:** Cold, dry air strips moisture from the skin surface. The skin compensates by producing more oil — leaving skin simultaneously dehydrated and oily (a very common combination in Amman).
- **UV exposure:** Jordan's high UV index triggers inflammation in the skin, which stimulates sebaceous gland activity.
- **Dust and pollution:** Amman's urban environment means pores are constantly exposed to particulate matter that mixes with sebum and causes congestion.

Understanding these factors explains why oily skin management in Amman requires a year-round strategy — not just summer mattifying products.

---

## Understanding What Causes Oily Skin

Before choosing treatments, it helps to understand what is actually driving your oiliness:

**Genetics:** Sebaceous gland activity is largely genetic. If your parents had oily skin, you likely will too. This does not mean it cannot be managed — but it sets the baseline.

**Hormones:** Androgens (testosterone and related hormones) directly stimulate sebaceous glands. This is why oiliness peaks during puberty, around menstruation, and with conditions like PCOS. Hormonal oiliness often requires a combined approach (professional treatment + medical management if needed).

**Over-cleansing and over-stripping:** Harsh cleansers, alcohol-based toners, and aggressive exfoliation remove the skin's natural lipid barrier. The skin responds by producing more oil to restore the barrier — creating a cycle of increasing oiliness.

**Dehydration:** Dehydrated skin (lacking water, not oil) triggers increased sebum production as a compensatory mechanism. Many oily skin clients are actually dehydrated — their skin is oily on the surface but lacks water in the deeper layers.

**Wrong skincare products:** Heavy, occlusive moisturisers and comedogenic (pore-blocking) ingredients trap oil and dead cells in pores, causing congestion and breakouts.

## The Best Professional Treatments for Oily Skin

### 1. Salicylic Acid Chemical Peel — The Most Targeted Treatment

Salicylic acid is a beta-hydroxy acid (BHA) — and its key property is that it is **oil-soluble**. Unlike AHAs (glycolic, lactic acid), which work on the skin surface, salicylic acid penetrates into the pore itself, dissolving the sebum and dead cell mixture that causes congestion, blackheads, and breakouts.

**What it does:**
- Dissolves sebum plugs and blackheads from within the pore
- Reduces pore size by clearing congestion
- Reduces inflammation (salicylic acid is also anti-inflammatory)
- Regulates sebum production over time with regular use
- Prevents new breakouts by keeping pores clear

**What to expect:**
- Mild tingling during application (5–10 minutes)
- Possible light flaking for 1–3 days
- Noticeably cleaner, less shiny skin within 48 hours
- Significant pore reduction and breakout control after 4–6 sessions

**Sessions:** 4–6 sessions spaced 3–4 weeks apart for best results. Monthly maintenance thereafter.

**Best for:** Oily skin, acne-prone skin, blackheads, enlarged pores, combination skin.

### 2. HydraFacial — Deep Cleansing Without Stripping

HydraFacial is particularly effective for oily skin because it addresses the root cause of congestion — clogged pores — without stripping or irritating the skin. The vortex suction extraction step physically removes blackheads, whiteheads, and sebum plugs from pores in a way that no home cleansing can replicate.

**What it does for oily skin:**
- Deep extraction of pore congestion (blackheads, sebum plugs)
- Gentle exfoliation without over-stripping
- Infusion of niacinamide and salicylic acid serums (customised for oily skin)
- Hydration — addressing the dehydration that drives compensatory oil production
- Immediate reduction in shine and visible pore size

**What to expect:**
- Zero downtime — skin looks cleaner and less shiny immediately
- Pores visibly smaller after extraction
- Skin feels balanced, not tight or dry

**Sessions:** Monthly maintenance is ideal. Can be combined with salicylic peels (alternating monthly).

### 3. RF Microneedling — Long-Term Pore Reduction

For clients with significantly enlarged pores and oily skin, RF microneedling is the most powerful long-term solution. The RF energy tightens the pore walls and the collagen remodelling response improves the surrounding skin structure — producing a genuine, lasting reduction in pore size.

**What it does:**
- Tightens pore walls through collagen stimulation
- Reduces sebaceous gland activity over time
- Improves overall skin texture and tone
- Addresses any acne scarring alongside pore reduction

**What to expect:**
- 3–4 sessions spaced 4 weeks apart
- 2–3 days of redness after each session
- Visible pore reduction from session 2–3
- Results continue improving for 3–4 months after the final session

**Best for:** Significantly enlarged pores, oily skin with acne scarring, clients who want long-term results rather than ongoing maintenance.

### 4. LED Blue Light Therapy — Targeting Acne-Causing Bacteria

Blue light (415nm wavelength) has a specific antibacterial effect on *Cutibacterium acnes* (formerly *Propionibacterium acnes*) — the bacteria responsible for inflammatory acne. It destroys the bacteria without any chemicals, heat, or irritation.

**What it does:**
- Kills acne-causing bacteria
- Reduces active breakouts
- Reduces inflammation
- No downtime, no irritation

**Best used as:** A complement to other treatments (HydraFacial, salicylic peel) rather than a standalone treatment. Particularly effective for clients with both oily skin and active breakouts.

## Treatments to Avoid with Oily Skin

### Overly Rich or Occlusive Facials

Facials that use heavy oils, thick creams, or occlusive masks can worsen congestion in oily skin. Always inform your specialist that you have oily skin so they can customise the products used.

### Aggressive Scrubs and Physical Exfoliation

Harsh physical scrubs create micro-tears in the skin and trigger inflammation — which stimulates more oil production. Chemical exfoliation (salicylic acid, gentle AHAs) is always preferable for oily skin.

### Steam-Only Facials

Steam opens pores temporarily but does nothing to clear the congestion inside them. Without proper extraction and treatment, pores simply close again with the same congestion inside.

## Home Care for Oily Skin in Amman

What you do between sessions determines how well your professional treatments hold. For oily skin:

### Morning Routine

1. **Gentle, foaming cleanser** — look for salicylic acid (0.5–2%) or niacinamide. Avoid harsh sulphate-heavy cleansers that strip the barrier.
2. **Niacinamide serum (10%)** — the single most effective ingredient for oily skin. Regulates sebum production, minimises pores, reduces redness. Apply after cleansing.
3. **Lightweight, oil-free moisturiser** — gel or fluid texture. Oily skin still needs hydration — skipping moisturiser makes oiliness worse.
4. **SPF 50+ (oil-free, non-comedogenic)** — gel or fluid SPF formulas work best for oily skin. Non-negotiable daily.

### Evening Routine

1. **Double cleanse** — oil cleanser first (counterintuitively, oil dissolves sebum effectively without stripping), then gentle foaming cleanser.
2. **BHA toner or essence (salicylic acid 1–2%)** — 2–3 nights per week to keep pores clear between sessions.
3. **Niacinamide serum** — every night.
4. **Lightweight moisturiser** — gel texture.
5. **Retinol (0.025–0.05%)** — 2 nights per week once your skin is accustomed. Retinol regulates sebaceous gland activity and is one of the most effective long-term oily skin ingredients.

### What to Avoid

- Alcohol-based toners — strip the barrier, trigger rebound oiliness
- Heavy, occlusive moisturisers (shea butter, petrolatum as primary ingredients)
- Skipping moisturiser — dehydrated skin produces more oil
- Over-cleansing (more than twice daily)
- Touching your face — transfers bacteria and stimulates oil production

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Will professional treatments permanently cure oily skin?**

No treatment permanently eliminates oily skin — sebaceous gland activity is largely genetic and hormonal. What professional treatments do is regulate oil production, keep pores clear, and significantly reduce shine and breakouts. With consistent maintenance, most clients achieve a dramatically improved baseline.

**Can I wear makeup after a salicylic peel?**

We recommend avoiding makeup for 24 hours after a peel. After that, non-comedogenic, oil-free formulas are fine. Avoid heavy foundations for 48 hours.

**My skin is oily in summer but dry in winter — what should I do?**

This is combination/seasonal skin — very common in Amman's climate. Your treatment protocol and home care routine should adapt seasonally. In summer, focus on oil control (salicylic peels, lightweight products). In winter, add more hydration (HydraFacial, hyaluronic acid serum) while maintaining oil regulation.

**Is oily skin more prone to ageing?**

Actually, oily skin ages more slowly than dry skin — sebum provides a natural moisturising effect that keeps the skin supple. The trade-off is congestion and breakouts in youth. With proper management, oily skin can be a long-term advantage.

**Can men get these treatments?**

Absolutely. Men's skin produces significantly more sebum than women's skin — making oily skin and enlarged pores even more common in male clients. All treatments are equally effective for men. See our [men's grooming treatments](/mens-grooming-amman) for more.
