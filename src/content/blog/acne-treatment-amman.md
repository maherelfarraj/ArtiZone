# Acne Treatment in Amman: The Most Effective Options for Clear Skin in 2026

Acne is one of the most common skin concerns we treat at ArtiZone — and one of the most misunderstood. Many clients arrive having tried every over-the-counter product on the market, only to find that their skin is still breaking out, scarring, or both.

The truth is that persistent acne almost always requires professional intervention. Clinic-based treatments work at a depth and precision that no cleanser or serum can match. This guide covers the most effective acne treatments available in Amman today, who they are for, and what results to realistically expect.

## Why Over-the-Counter Products Often Fail

Most drugstore acne products contain low concentrations of active ingredients — salicylic acid at 0.5–2%, benzoyl peroxide at 2.5–5%. These can help with mild, occasional breakouts, but they rarely address the root causes of persistent acne:

- **Excess sebum production** driven by hormones or genetics
- **Clogged pores** with deep congestion that surface cleansers cannot reach
- **Bacterial overgrowth** (C. acnes) deep within the follicle
- **Inflammation** that leads to cysts and nodules
- **Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation** — the dark marks left after breakouts heal

Professional treatments target all of these simultaneously, with clinical-strength actives and technology that penetrates far deeper than anything available over the counter.

## The Most Effective Clinic-Based Acne Treatments

### 1. Salicylic Acid Chemical Peel

Salicylic acid is oil-soluble, which means it penetrates directly into pores — making it uniquely effective for acne-prone skin. A professional salicylic peel uses concentrations of 20–30%, far beyond what is available in retail products.

**What it does:**
- Dissolves the keratin plugs that block pores
- Reduces sebum production
- Has natural anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties
- Accelerates cell turnover to fade post-acne marks

**Best for:** Active breakouts, blackheads, whiteheads, oily skin, mild-to-moderate acne

**Downtime:** Mild peeling for 3–5 days. Most clients see clearer skin within 1–2 weeks.

**Sessions needed:** 4–6 sessions spaced 2–3 weeks apart for best results.

---

### 2. HydraFacial with Acne Protocol

HydraFacial is one of the few facial treatments safe for active acne. The standard protocol is adapted for acne-prone skin with:

- A salicylic acid peel step to loosen congestion
- Vortex extraction to remove blackheads and impurities without manual squeezing (which spreads bacteria)
- Blue LED light therapy add-on to kill acne-causing bacteria
- Clarifying and balancing serums instead of heavy hydrators

**What it does:**
- Deep-cleans pores without aggravating breakouts
- Reduces active inflammation
- Balances sebum production
- Leaves skin clearer and calmer immediately

**Best for:** Active acne, congested skin, clients who need results with zero downtime

**Downtime:** None. Safe to return to normal activities immediately.

---

### 3. LED Light Therapy (Blue + Red)

LED therapy uses specific wavelengths of light to target acne at the bacterial and cellular level — with no heat, no chemicals, and no downtime.

- **Blue LED (415nm):** Kills *Cutibacterium acnes* (the bacteria responsible for inflammatory acne) by activating porphyrins within the bacteria
- **Red LED (630nm):** Reduces inflammation, accelerates healing, and stimulates collagen to repair acne damage

**What it does:**
- Reduces active breakouts without any irritation
- Calms redness and inflammation
- Accelerates healing of existing spots
- Safe for all skin types, including sensitive and reactive skin

**Best for:** Inflammatory acne, cystic acne, sensitive skin that cannot tolerate peels, maintenance between other treatments

**Downtime:** None. Can be combined with other treatments in the same session.

---

### 4. Microneedling for Acne Scars

Once active acne is under control, many clients are left with scarring — atrophic (pitted) scars, rolling scars, or boxcar scars. Microneedling is one of the most effective treatments for remodelling acne scar tissue.

Microneedling creates controlled micro-injuries in the skin, triggering the body's natural wound-healing response. This stimulates collagen and elastin production, gradually filling and smoothing depressed scars.

**What it does:**
- Stimulates collagen remodelling in scar tissue
- Improves skin texture and reduces the depth of pitted scars
- Evens out skin tone and reduces post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
- Can be combined with vitamin C or growth factor serums for enhanced results

**Best for:** Atrophic acne scars, rolling scars, boxcar scars, uneven skin texture post-acne

**Downtime:** 3–5 days of redness and mild sensitivity

**Sessions needed:** 3–6 sessions spaced 4 weeks apart

---

### 5. Glycolic Acid Peel

Glycolic acid is water-soluble and works primarily on the skin's surface, making it excellent for post-acne pigmentation and overall skin renewal.

**What it does:**
- Accelerates cell turnover to fade dark marks left by acne
- Smooths skin texture
- Brightens dull, congested skin
- Mild antibacterial effect

**Best for:** Post-acne hyperpigmentation, mild acne, dull skin, combination skin types

**Downtime:** Mild sensitivity for 1–2 days, possible light flaking

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I get a facial if I have active acne?**
Yes — but not all facials are appropriate. HydraFacial with acne protocol and LED therapy are both safe for active breakouts. Avoid steam facials, manual extractions by untrained staff, or heavy oil-based treatments during a breakout.

**Will a chemical peel make my acne worse?**
Some clients experience a brief purging phase in the first 1–2 weeks as the peel accelerates cell turnover and brings congestion to the surface. This is normal and temporary. After the purging phase, skin typically clears significantly.

**How many sessions do I need?**
For mild acne: 3–4 sessions. For moderate-to-severe acne: 6–10 sessions over 3–4 months. Your specialist will assess your skin and recommend a realistic plan at your consultation.

**Is acne treatment safe for darker skin tones?**
Yes — with the right protocols. Darker skin tones (common in Jordan and the Arab world) are more prone to post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, so we use gentler peels and avoid aggressive treatments that could worsen pigmentation. Our specialists are experienced with all skin tones.

**Can men get acne treatment?**
Absolutely. Acne is equally common in men, and all our acne treatments are available for male clients. Men's skin tends to be oilier and thicker, which can make acne more stubborn — but also responds very well to professional treatment.
