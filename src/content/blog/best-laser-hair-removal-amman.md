The results are long-lasting, cost-effective over time, and — when done correctly — completely safe for all skin types.

## What Separates a Great Laser Clinic from an Average One

Not all laser hair removal is equal. Here is what actually matters when choosing a clinic in Amman.

### 1. The Technology They Use

There are several types of laser used for hair removal. The most effective and safest for a wide range of skin tones — including the olive and darker skin tones common in Jordan — is the **Nd:YAG laser** or a **diode laser** with proper settings for darker skin.

> **Ask any clinic:** "Which laser technology do you use, and is it suitable for my skin tone?" If they cannot answer clearly, walk away.

Outdated IPL (Intense Pulsed Light) machines are often marketed as "laser" but are less precise, less effective, and carry higher risk of burns on darker skin.

### 2. Certified, Trained Practitioners

Laser hair removal is a medical-grade aesthetic procedure. It should only be performed by trained, certified practitioners — not untrained staff following a script.

At ArtiZone, every laser treatment is performed by certified specialists with hands-on training in laser physics, skin anatomy, and safety protocols.

### 3. A Proper Skin Assessment Before Treatment

A reputable clinic will always conduct a **consultation and patch test** before your first session. This assesses:

- Your skin type (Fitzpatrick scale I–VI)
- Hair color and texture
- Any contraindications (medications, hormonal conditions, recent sun exposure)
- The correct laser settings for your specific profile

Skipping this step is a red flag.

### 4. Transparent Pricing

Laser hair removal pricing in Amman varies enormously. Be cautious of:

- Extremely low prices (often signal outdated equipment or unqualified staff)
- Hidden fees added after the initial quote
- Pressure to buy large packages upfront before a trial session

A trustworthy clinic will give you a clear price per session or per package, explain exactly what is included, and let you start with a single session before committing.

### 5. Hygiene and Safety Standards

Every session involves direct skin contact with a device. The clinic should use:

- Single-use gel applicators or properly sterilized tips
- Clean, fresh linens for every client
- Protective eyewear for both client and practitioner
- Cooling mechanisms to protect the skin surface

## Before Your Session: How to Prepare

To get the best results and avoid complications:

- **Shave** the treatment area 24 hours before (do not wax or thread — the root must be intact)
- **Avoid sun exposure** for 2 weeks before treatment
- **No self-tanner** for at least 2 weeks prior
- **Skip retinoids and exfoliants** for 3–5 days before
- **Stay hydrated** — well-hydrated skin responds better to laser

## After Your Session: What to Expect

- Mild redness and warmth for 2–24 hours — completely normal
- Hair will appear to "shed" over the following 1–3 weeks (this is the treated hair falling out)
- Avoid sun exposure and apply SPF 50+ daily
- No hot showers, saunas, or intense exercise for 24 hours
- Do not wax or thread between sessions — shaving is fine

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is laser hair removal painful?**
Most clients describe it as a mild snapping sensation — similar to a rubber band flick. Modern lasers with built-in cooling systems make the treatment very comfortable. Sensitive areas like the bikini line may feel more intense, but sessions are short.

**Is it safe for darker skin tones?**
Yes — with the right laser. Nd:YAG lasers are specifically designed for darker skin tones (Fitzpatrick IV–VI) and are safe when used by a trained practitioner. Always confirm the clinic uses appropriate technology for your skin type.

**Can I get laser during summer?**
You can, but you must be diligent about sun avoidance and SPF. Many clients prefer to start in autumn or winter when sun exposure is lower, so they are hair-free by summer.

**How long do results last?**
After a full course, most clients experience permanent hair reduction of 80–95%. Some hair may regrow over years due to hormonal changes, but it is typically finer and lighter. Occasional maintenance sessions keep results optimal.
