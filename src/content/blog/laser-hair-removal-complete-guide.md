# Laser Hair Removal: Everything You Need to Know

Laser hair removal is one of the most popular aesthetic treatments in the world — and for good reason. It offers a long-term solution to unwanted hair that's far more effective than shaving, waxing, or threading. But if you've never had it done before, you probably have questions.

This guide answers everything you need to know before booking your first session.

## How Does Laser Hair Removal Work?

Laser hair removal uses concentrated beams of light that target the melanin (pigment) in your hair follicles. The light energy converts to heat, which damages the follicle and inhibits future hair growth — without harming the surrounding skin.

The key word is *inhibits*. Laser hair removal significantly reduces hair growth and, over multiple sessions, can result in permanent hair reduction. Most clients see 80–90% reduction after a full course of treatment.

## Which Areas Can Be Treated?

Laser hair removal can be performed on virtually any area of the body, including:

- **Face:** Upper lip, chin, sideburns, full face
- **Body:** Underarms, arms, legs, back, chest, stomach
- **Bikini area:** Bikini line, Brazilian, full bikini
- **Sensitive areas:** Neck, shoulders, feet and toes

At ArtiZone, we offer laser hair removal for both women and men across all body areas.

## How Many Sessions Do You Need?

This is the most common question — and the honest answer is: it depends. Hair grows in cycles, and laser only affects follicles in the active growth phase. Because not all hairs are in the same phase at the same time, multiple sessions are needed to catch each follicle.

**Typical session counts by area:**

| Area | Sessions Needed |
|------|----------------|
| Underarms | 6–8 sessions |
| Bikini / Brazilian | 6–10 sessions |
| Legs (full) | 8–10 sessions |
| Face | 6–8 sessions |
| Back / Chest | 8–12 sessions |

Sessions are spaced 4–8 weeks apart depending on the area and your hair growth cycle.

## Does It Hurt?

Most clients describe the sensation as a rubber band snapping against the skin — brief and tolerable. Modern laser systems are significantly more comfortable than older technology, and many include built-in cooling to minimise discomfort.

Pain tolerance varies by area. The bikini line and upper lip tend to be more sensitive. If you're concerned, ask about topical numbing cream — we can apply it before your session.

## How to Prepare for Your Session

Getting the most from your laser treatment starts before you arrive. Follow these preparation steps:

### Do:
- **Shave the area** 24 hours before your appointment (not wax or thread — the hair root must be intact)
- **Avoid sun exposure** for 2 weeks before treatment
- **Cleanse the area** thoroughly on the day of your appointment
- **Wear loose, comfortable clothing** to your appointment

### Don't:
- **Wax, thread, or use depilatory creams** for at least 4 weeks before treatment (these remove the hair root that laser targets)
- **Apply self-tanner or fake tan** for 2 weeks before
- **Use retinol or AHA/BHA** on the treatment area for 3–5 days before
- **Expose treated skin to sun** for 2 weeks after treatment

## What to Expect After Treatment

Immediately after your session, the treated area may appear slightly red and feel like mild sunburn. This is completely normal and typically subsides within a few hours.

Over the following 1–3 weeks, you'll notice hairs shedding from the treated follicles — this is the laser working. Don't pick or pull them; let them fall out naturally.

**Post-treatment care:**
- Apply aloe vera gel or a soothing moisturiser
- Avoid hot showers, saunas, and exercise for 24–48 hours
- Use SPF 50+ on any treated areas exposed to sun
- Avoid tight clothing on treated areas for 24 hours

## Who Is a Good Candidate?

Laser hair removal works best on people with a contrast between hair colour and skin tone — traditionally, dark hair on lighter skin. However, modern laser technology has advanced significantly and can now safely treat a much wider range of skin tones.

At ArtiZone, we use advanced laser systems suitable for all skin types, including darker skin tones common in the Middle East. Our specialists will assess your skin and hair type during a consultation to recommend the most appropriate settings.

**Laser may not be suitable if you:**
- Are pregnant or breastfeeding
- Have certain skin conditions in the treatment area
- Are taking photosensitising medications
- Have very light, blonde, grey, or red hair (minimal melanin for the laser to target)
