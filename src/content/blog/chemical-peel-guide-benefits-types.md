# Chemical Peels: Everything You Need to Know Before Your First Treatment

Chemical peels have been used in aesthetic medicine for decades — and for good reason. When performed correctly, they deliver results that few other treatments can match: smoother texture, reduced pigmentation, clearer pores, fewer fine lines, and a genuine glow that lasts.

Yet many people hesitate because they don't fully understand what a chemical peel involves. This guide answers every question — from how peels work to which type is right for your skin concerns.

## How Chemical Peels Work

A chemical peel applies a carefully formulated acid solution to the skin for a controlled period of time. The acid breaks down the bonds between dead skin cells, causing the outer layers to shed. This process:

1. **Removes the dull, damaged outer layer** — revealing fresher, younger-looking skin beneath
2. **Stimulates cell turnover** — accelerating the production of new skin cells
3. **Triggers collagen production** — the controlled injury response stimulates the dermis to produce new collagen and elastin
4. **Clears congestion** — acids penetrate pores to dissolve sebum and dead cell buildup

The depth of the peel determines how dramatic the results are — and how much downtime is involved.

## Types of Chemical Peels

### Superficial Peels (Light Peels)

**Acids used:** Glycolic acid (AHA), lactic acid (AHA), mandelic acid, salicylic acid (BHA)

**Depth:** Epidermis only (outermost layer)

**Downtime:** None to minimal — mild redness for a few hours, possible light flaking over 2–3 days

**Best for:**
- Dull, tired-looking skin
- Mild uneven skin tone
- Dry or dehydrated skin
- Mild acne and congestion
- Maintenance and regular skin health

**What to expect:** A tingling or mild stinging sensation during application. Skin may look slightly pink immediately after. Most people return to normal activities the same day. Results are subtle but cumulative — a course of 4–6 peels delivers the best outcome.

**Frequency:** Every 2–4 weeks

---

### Medium Peels

**Acids used:** Trichloroacetic acid (TCA) 15–35%, Jessner's solution, combination peels

**Depth:** Epidermis and upper dermis

**Downtime:** 5–7 days of visible peeling and redness

**Best for:**
- Moderate sun damage and pigmentation
- Fine to moderate lines and wrinkles
- Acne scars (mild to moderate)
- Uneven skin tone and texture
- Melasma (with caution)

**What to expect:** More significant frosting (whitening) of the skin during application. Skin will feel tight and look red for 1–2 days, followed by visible peeling for 4–7 days. Results are noticeably more dramatic than superficial peels — smoother texture, reduced pigmentation, and improved firmness.

**Frequency:** Every 3–6 months

---

### Deep Peels

**Acids used:** Phenol, high-concentration TCA (50%+)

**Depth:** Deep dermis

**Downtime:** 2–3 weeks

**Best for:**
- Significant sun damage
- Deep wrinkles and lines
- Severe acne scarring
- Significant skin laxity

**Note:** Deep peels require careful patient selection and are performed under controlled conditions. They are not suitable for all skin types — particularly darker skin tones, where the risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation is higher.

## Which Peel Is Right for You?

| Concern | Recommended Peel |
|---------|-----------------|
| Dull skin, no downtime | Superficial (glycolic or lactic) |
| Acne and congestion | Superficial (salicylic acid) |
| Mild pigmentation | Superficial to medium |
| Moderate sun damage | Medium (TCA) |
| Fine lines | Superficial to medium |
| Moderate acne scars | Medium (TCA or Jessner's) |
| Significant ageing | Medium to deep |

## Chemical Peels for Different Skin Tones

One of the most important considerations with chemical peels is skin tone. Darker skin tones (Fitzpatrick types IV–VI) are at higher risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH) — darkening of the skin in response to the controlled injury of a peel.

This doesn't mean chemical peels aren't suitable for darker skin — it means the right peel must be chosen carefully:

- **Mandelic acid peels** are particularly well-suited to darker skin tones due to their larger molecular size and gentler action
- **Salicylic acid peels** are safe and effective for all skin tones
- **TCA peels** can be used on darker skin at lower concentrations with proper preparation
- **Phenol peels** are generally not recommended for darker skin tones

## Preparing for a Chemical Peel

Proper preparation improves results and reduces the risk of complications:

**2–4 weeks before:**
- Begin using a retinol or retinoid (if not already) to prime cell turnover
- Use SPF 50 daily — sun-damaged skin responds less predictably to peels
- If you have darker skin, your specialist may recommend a brightening agent (hydroquinone or kojic acid) to reduce PIH risk

**1 week before:**
- Stop using retinoids, AHAs, BHAs, and any exfoliating products
- Avoid waxing, threading, or laser treatments on the treatment area
- Do not use self-tanner

**Day of treatment:**
- Arrive with clean, product-free skin
- Avoid sun exposure immediately before your appointment

## What to Expect During Treatment

1. **Cleansing:** Your skin is thoroughly cleansed to remove all traces of oil and product
2. **Degreasing:** A degreasing solution is applied to ensure even acid penetration
3. **Peel application:** The acid solution is applied evenly using a brush or gauze
4. **Timing:** The peel is left on for a precisely timed period (varies by peel type and skin response)
5. **Neutralisation:** Superficial and medium peels are neutralised with a bicarbonate solution; deep peels self-neutralise
6. **Post-peel care:** A soothing serum and SPF are applied

The entire process takes 30–60 minutes depending on the peel type.

---

## Aftercare: How to Get the Best Results

Post-peel care is as important as the treatment itself. Follow these guidelines carefully:

**Immediately after:**
- Apply the recommended post-peel moisturiser generously and frequently
- Do not touch, pick, or peel the skin — let it shed naturally
- Avoid all active ingredients (retinol, AHAs, BHAs, Vitamin C) until skin has fully healed
- Apply SPF 50 every morning — UV exposure post-peel can cause permanent pigmentation

**During peeling phase:**
- Keep skin well moisturised at all times
- Use a gentle, fragrance-free cleanser
- Avoid exercise and activities that cause sweating for 48 hours
- Do not use hot water on the face

**Long-term:**
- Maintain results with regular SPF use
- Book maintenance peels every 4–8 weeks (superficial) or every 3–6 months (medium)
- Continue a consistent home skincare routine

---

## How Many Peels Do You Need?

This depends on your concern and the peel type:

- **Maintenance and glow:** 1 superficial peel per month
- **Acne and congestion:** Course of 4–6 salicylic acid peels, 2–3 weeks apart
- **Pigmentation:** Course of 4–6 medium peels, 4–6 weeks apart
- **Anti-aging:** 2–3 medium peels per year, with monthly superficial peels in between

Results are cumulative — each peel builds on the last. Consistency is key.
