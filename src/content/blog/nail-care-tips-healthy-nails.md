# 10 Tips for Stronger, Healthier Nails

Your nails say a lot about your overall health — and they deserve just as much care as your skin. Whether you struggle with brittle, peeling nails or simply want to maintain a polished, healthy look, these expert tips will make a real difference.

## 1. Keep Nails Clean and Dry

Moisture is the enemy of nail health when it's trapped under the nail. Prolonged exposure to water — from washing dishes, swimming, or long baths — weakens the nail plate and makes nails prone to splitting and peeling.

**What to do:**
- Wear rubber gloves when washing dishes or cleaning
- Dry hands and nails thoroughly after washing
- Avoid soaking nails for extended periods

## 2. File in One Direction

How you file your nails matters more than most people realise. Sawing back and forth with a nail file creates micro-tears in the nail edge, leading to splitting and peeling.

**The right technique:**
- File in one direction only — from the outer edge toward the centre
- Use a fine-grit file (not a coarse metal file)
- File when nails are dry, not after soaking (wet nails are more fragile)

## 3. Moisturise Your Nails and Cuticles Daily

Nails need hydration just like skin. Dry, dehydrated nails are brittle and prone to breaking. Your cuticles — the thin skin at the base of each nail — also need regular moisture to stay healthy and prevent painful hangnails.

**Best practice:**
- Apply cuticle oil or a rich hand cream every night before bed
- Massage it into the nail plate and cuticle area
- Look for products containing jojoba oil, vitamin E, or argan oil

## 4. Never Cut Your Cuticles

Cutting cuticles is a common salon practice, but it's actually harmful. Cuticles exist to protect the nail matrix (where new nail cells are produced) from bacteria and infection. Cutting them removes this protection and can lead to infections, inflammation, and even permanent nail damage.

**Instead:** Gently push cuticles back with a wooden orange stick after softening them with cuticle oil or warm water.

## 5. Eat for Nail Health

Nail strength starts from within. Brittle, slow-growing nails are often a sign of nutritional deficiency. The most important nutrients for nail health are:

- **Biotin (Vitamin B7):** Found in eggs, nuts, seeds, and sweet potato. Biotin is the most well-researched nutrient for nail strength.
- **Protein:** Nails are made of keratin, a protein. Adequate protein intake is essential for nail growth.
- **Iron:** Iron deficiency is a common cause of brittle nails. Found in red meat, lentils, and leafy greens.
- **Zinc:** Supports nail growth and repair. Found in pumpkin seeds, chickpeas, and meat.
- **Omega-3 fatty acids:** Keep nails hydrated from the inside. Found in fatty fish, walnuts, and flaxseed.

## 6. Give Your Nails a Break from Polish

As beautiful as nail polish is, wearing it continuously without breaks can weaken and discolour nails. Polish prevents nails from "breathing" and can cause dehydration and yellowing over time.

**What to do:**
- Take a 1–2 week break from polish every 2–3 months
- During your break, apply a strengthening base coat or nail treatment
- Always use a base coat before applying coloured polish to prevent staining

## 7. Use Acetone-Free Nail Polish Remover

Acetone is highly effective at removing polish, but it's also extremely drying. Regular use strips nails of their natural oils and moisture, leaving them brittle and prone to breaking.

Switch to an acetone-free remover for everyday use. It takes slightly longer to work, but your nails will thank you.

## 8. Protect Nails from Harsh Chemicals

Cleaning products, detergents, and even some hand sanitisers contain chemicals that damage nails and dry out the surrounding skin. Frequent exposure breaks down the nail plate and strips natural oils.

**Simple protection:**
- Wear gloves for cleaning and gardening
- Rinse hands thoroughly after using hand sanitiser
- Apply hand cream after any chemical exposure

## 9. Don't Use Nails as Tools

Using your nails to open cans, peel stickers, or scratch surfaces is one of the fastest ways to cause breakage. Nails are not tools — treat them like the delicate structures they are.

Keep a small tool (like a pen cap or coin) in your bag for tasks that would otherwise tempt you to use your nails.

## When to See a Specialist

Some nail changes can indicate underlying health conditions. See a doctor if you notice:

- Significant colour changes (yellow, green, black, or white spots)
- Nails separating from the nail bed
- Pitting or ridging that appears suddenly
- Swelling or pain around the nail
