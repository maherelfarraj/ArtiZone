# Best Acne Scar Treatment in Amman: What Actually Works in 2026

Acne scars are one of the most emotionally difficult skin concerns to live with — and one of the most frequently mistreated. The wrong treatment for the wrong scar type can waste months of time and money with minimal results. The right treatment, matched precisely to your scar type, can produce genuinely life-changing improvement.

This guide covers the best acne scar treatments available in Amman in 2026, how to identify which type of scars you have, and what realistic results look like for each.

## First: Understand Your Scar Type

Not all acne scars are the same. The most important step before any treatment is correct classification — because ice pick scars, boxcar scars, rolling scars, and post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH) each respond to different treatments.

### Ice Pick Scars

Ice pick scars are deep, narrow, pitted depressions that extend into the dermis. They look like small puncture marks and are most common on the cheeks. They are the most challenging type to treat because of their depth.

**Best treatments:** RF microneedling, TCA Cross technique (a targeted chemical peel applied directly into the scar channel).

### Boxcar Scars

Boxcar scars are broad, box-shaped depressions with sharp, defined edges. They are typically wider than ice pick scars and most common on the cheeks and temples.

**Best treatments:** RF microneedling, microneedling with PRP, medium-depth chemical peels.

### Rolling Scars

Rolling scars create a wave-like, undulating texture across the skin. They are caused by fibrous bands of tissue tethering the skin to deeper structures, creating the characteristic rolling appearance.

**Best treatments:** RF microneedling, subcision (a minor procedure that releases the fibrous bands), followed by collagen-stimulating treatments.

### Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation (PIH)

PIH is not a true scar — it is flat discolouration (dark marks) left after acne heals. It is caused by excess melanin production triggered by inflammation. It is extremely common in olive and darker skin tones, including many Jordanian and Arab skin types.

**Best treatments:** Chemical peels (glycolic, lactic, mandelic), brightening facials, vitamin C serums, and strict daily SPF use.

## The Best Acne Scar Treatments in Amman

### 1. RF Microneedling — The Gold Standard for Atrophic Scars

RF microneedling (radiofrequency microneedling) is widely considered the most effective non-surgical treatment for depressed acne scars. It combines two proven mechanisms in a single treatment: the collagen-stimulating effect of microneedling and the deep tissue remodelling effect of radiofrequency energy.

**How it works:** Fine needles create micro-channels in the skin while simultaneously delivering RF energy at precise depths (1.5mm–3.5mm). This dual action triggers an intense collagen and elastin synthesis response — gradually filling and smoothing depressed scars from within.

**What to expect:**
- 3–6 sessions spaced 4 weeks apart
- 2–4 days of redness and mild swelling after each session
- Visible improvement from session 2–3
- Full results at 3–4 months after the final session
- 60–80% improvement in scar depth and texture is typical

**Safe for all skin tones:** Unlike some laser treatments, RF microneedling carries no risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation on darker skin tones — making it the preferred choice for olive and darker complexions common in Jordan.

### 2. Microneedling (Collagen Induction Therapy)

Standard microneedling without RF is a gentler option that still produces meaningful results for shallow boxcar and rolling scars. It works by creating controlled micro-injuries that trigger the body's natural wound-healing response, flooding the area with collagen and elastin.

**Best for:** Shallow scars, skin texture, PIH, fine lines, and as a maintenance treatment between RF microneedling sessions.

**What to expect:**
- 4–6 sessions spaced 3–4 weeks apart
- 2–3 days of redness
- Progressive improvement over 3–4 months

### 3. Chemical Peels — Essential for PIH and Shallow Scars

Chemical peels are the most effective treatment for post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation and shallow surface scars. Different acids work at different depths:

- **Glycolic acid (AHA):** Superficial peel, ideal for PIH, dullness, and mild texture. Minimal downtime.
- **Lactic acid:** Gentler than glycolic, excellent for sensitive skin and mild PIH.
- **Mandelic acid:** The best choice for darker skin tones — larger molecular size means slower, more controlled penetration with lower risk of irritation.
- **TCA (trichloroacetic acid):** Medium-depth peel for more significant resurfacing. 5–7 days of peeling. Most effective for shallow boxcar scars and moderate PIH.

**A note on darker skin tones:** Aggressive peels (high-concentration TCA, phenol) carry a real risk of PIH in olive and darker skin. At ArtiZone, we use lower-concentration peels with gradual build-up protocols specifically designed for Jordanian and Arab skin types.

### 4. Laser Resurfacing

Fractional laser resurfacing creates thousands of micro-treatment zones in the skin, triggering intensive collagen remodelling. It is highly effective for moderate-to-severe scarring but requires more downtime than RF microneedling.

**What to expect:**
- 2–4 sessions
- 5–10 days of downtime (redness, peeling, sensitivity)
- Significant improvement in scar depth and texture
- Results continue improving for 3–6 months post-treatment

**Important for darker skin tones:** Laser resurfacing requires careful selection of wavelength and settings for olive and darker skin. Ablative lasers carry a higher risk of PIH in darker skin types. Non-ablative fractional lasers are generally safer. Always consult a specialist experienced with your skin tone.

## What About Home Treatments?

Home skincare cannot replace clinical treatments for structural scars — but it plays an important supporting role:

- **Vitamin C serum (10–20%):** Inhibits melanin production, helps fade PIH, and supports collagen synthesis. Apply every morning.
- **Retinol or retinoid:** Accelerates cell turnover and stimulates collagen. Start low (0.025–0.05%) and build up. Use at night only.
- **Niacinamide (5–10%):** Reduces PIH, minimises pores, and strengthens the skin barrier.
- **SPF 50+ every day:** Non-negotiable. UV exposure darkens PIH dramatically and slows all scar healing. This is the single most important thing you can do between clinic sessions.
- **Avoid picking:** Every time you pick at a spot, you deepen the inflammatory response and increase the risk of scarring.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I treat active acne and acne scars at the same time?**

Yes — many of our treatments address both simultaneously. Microneedling, chemical peels, and LED therapy all help with active acne while also beginning the scar remodelling process. However, we recommend getting active breakouts under control first if possible, to prevent new scars from forming during treatment.

**Is acne scar treatment painful?**

RF microneedling involves mild discomfort — we apply topical numbing cream 30 minutes before the session, which makes it very tolerable for most clients. Chemical peels cause a mild stinging sensation during application. Most clients find treatments very manageable.

**How long do results last?**

Scar improvement from RF microneedling and laser resurfacing is permanent — the collagen remodelling that fills and smooths scars does not reverse. PIH can recur if sun protection is not maintained. Maintenance sessions (1–2 per year) help preserve and extend results.

**Is acne scar treatment safe for all skin tones?**

Yes — when the right protocols are used. We specialise in treating olive and darker skin tones, which are the most common in Jordan and the Arab world. We use protocols specifically designed to minimise the risk of PIH in melanin-rich skin.
