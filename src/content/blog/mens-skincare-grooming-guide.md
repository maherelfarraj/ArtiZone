# Men's Skincare: The No-Nonsense Guide to Better Skin

Skincare isn't just for women. Men's skin faces the same environmental stressors, ageing, and sun damage — and in many ways, it faces more. Daily shaving, thicker skin prone to clogged pores, and the tendency to skip moisturiser all add up over time.

The good news: a simple, consistent routine makes a significant difference. And for deeper concerns, professional treatments designed specifically for men deliver results that no home routine can match.

## How Men's Skin Differs

Men's skin is biologically different from women's in several important ways:

- **Thicker:** Men's skin is approximately 25% thicker due to higher testosterone levels, which means it ages differently — but not necessarily better.
- **Oilier:** Men have more active sebaceous (oil) glands, making them more prone to enlarged pores, blackheads, and acne.
- **More collagen:** Men have higher collagen density, which delays the appearance of fine lines — but when ageing does show, it tends to happen more rapidly.
- **Shaving stress:** Daily shaving causes micro-trauma to the skin, leading to irritation, ingrown hairs, and sensitivity if not properly managed.

Understanding these differences means your skincare routine needs to be tailored to your skin's specific needs — not just borrowed from someone else's shelf.

## The Essential Men's Skincare Routine

You don't need 10 products. Start with these four steps and you'll be ahead of 90% of men.

### Step 1: Cleanser (Morning & Evening)

Wash your face twice a day with a cleanser designed for your skin type. Bar soap is too harsh — it strips your skin's natural oils and disrupts the pH balance.

- **Oily/acne-prone skin:** Gel or foaming cleanser with salicylic acid
- **Normal/combination skin:** Gentle gel cleanser
- **Dry or sensitive skin:** Cream cleanser or micellar water

If you shave in the morning, cleanse before shaving — clean skin reduces irritation and gives a closer shave.

### Step 2: Moisturiser

This is the step most men skip — and the one that makes the biggest difference. Moisturiser keeps your skin barrier healthy, reduces the appearance of fine lines, and prevents the overproduction of oil that leads to breakouts.

Apply after cleansing while skin is still slightly damp. Choose a lightweight gel moisturiser if you're oily, or a richer cream if you're dry.

### Step 3: SPF (Every Morning)

Sun damage is the number one cause of premature ageing, dark spots, and uneven skin tone. Most men never wear SPF — which is exactly why their skin often looks older than it should.

Use a broad-spectrum SPF 30–50 every morning. Many moisturisers now include SPF, making this step easy to combine.

### Step 4: Post-Shave Care

After shaving, apply a soothing aftershave balm (not alcohol-based splash — that dries and irritates). Look for ingredients like aloe vera, niacinamide, or centella asiatica to calm redness and prevent ingrown hairs.

## Common Men's Skin Concerns & How to Fix Them

### Ingrown Hairs
Caused by shaving against the grain or with a dull blade. Fix: exfoliate 2–3 times per week, shave with the grain, and use a sharp, clean blade. For persistent ingrown hairs, laser hair removal is the permanent solution.

### Enlarged Pores & Blackheads
Men's oilier skin makes clogged pores a common issue. Fix: use a salicylic acid cleanser, exfoliate regularly, and consider a professional HydraFacial or deep-cleansing facial every 4–6 weeks.

### Dull, Tired-Looking Skin
Often caused by dehydration, lack of sleep, and sun damage. Fix: drink more water, use a Vitamin C serum in the morning, and book a professional brightening facial.

### Acne & Breakouts
Hormonal fluctuations, stress, and oily skin all contribute. Fix: consistent cleansing, non-comedogenic (non-pore-blocking) moisturiser, and professional treatments like chemical peels or LED blue light therapy for persistent acne.

### Fine Lines & Loss of Firmness
Starts appearing in the mid-30s for most men. Fix: retinol serum at night, SPF every day, and professional treatments like microneedling or radiofrequency for more significant concerns.
